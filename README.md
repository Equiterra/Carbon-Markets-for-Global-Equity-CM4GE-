# Carbon-Markets-for-Global-Equity-CM4GE-
Carbon Markets for Global Equity (CM4GE): A sovereign-aligned framework for climate finance and carbon markets under the Paris Agreement.

CM4GE is a framework designed to reposition carbon markets as a core component of sovereign climate finance architecture.

It proposes a transition from fragmented project-based mechanisms toward integrated, scalable, and equity-aligned financial systems.

- Environmental integrity (data-backed MRV)
- Financial scalability
- Equity and value redistribution
- Market infrastructure development

## Scope
- Article 6 mechanisms
- Voluntary and compliance carbon markets
- Adaptation-linked finance
- Digital MRV systems

## Objective
To bridge climate outcomes with capital markets while ensuring equitable value distribution across the Global South.

# Article 6 — The Global Settlement Layer

## Concept

Article 6 of the Paris Agreement introduces the accounting infrastructure required for global carbon markets.

It enables:
- transfer of mitigation outcomes across borders  
- prevention of double counting  
- integration with national climate commitments  

---

## Core Mechanisms

### Article 6.2 — Cooperative Approaches
- Bilateral / multilateral agreements  
- ITMO transfers  
- Flexible market structures  

---

### Article 6.4 — Centralized Mechanism
- UN-governed system  
- Standardized methodologies  
- Global credibility layer  

---

## Corresponding Adjustments

Key function:
- ensures emissions reductions are counted only once  

This transforms carbon into:
> **Sovereign-accounted assets**

---

## Strategic Role

Article 6 functions as:

> **The settlement infrastructure of global carbon markets**

Equivalent to:
- clearing systems in financial markets  

---

## Implication

Carbon markets evolve from:
- fragmented systems  

to:
- **globally interoperable financial infrastructure**
